# NOT THE MOON RECORDS — Musical Exclusion Document

This document defines what The Moon Records does not release. As binding as the signing criteria.

---

## THE FUNDAMENTAL EXCLUSION

**Any music with a beat.**

Not "mostly beatless." Not "the beats drop out in the middle." Not "it's ambient with a subtle pulse." If the music has a tempo structure — a kick pattern, a rhythmic foundation, a pulse the body responds to — it is not for this label. This is not a quality judgment. It is a definition.

The same track at half the tempo, stripped of its rhythmic architecture, reconsidered from the ground up as a beatless work: that track may belong here. The original does not.

---

## SOUNDS THAT DO NOT BELONG

**Ambient as garnish**
Atmospheric passages in otherwise rhythmic or melodic work. Interludes. Bridges. The quiet part before the drop. Music that uses ambient as texture within a larger structure that is not ambient. The Moon Records releases work that is ambient in its entirety, not work that passes through ambient on the way somewhere else.

**Meditation and wellness ambient**
Music produced to induce a state — relaxation, focus, sleep, creativity. The difference between this and music that happens to be calming is intention. Wellness ambient is functional. It wants to produce a result in the listener. The Moon Records is not a service. The music here is not designed to do something to you. It exists. What you do with it is your business.

**New Age resolution**
Music in which everything resolves. Long pads that arrive at a major chord and hold it. Drones that slowly brighten toward warmth. The sense that the music is leading somewhere comfortable, somewhere safe. This aesthetic is a legitimate form. It is not this label's form. The Moon Records is not in the business of comfort.

**Lo-fi as costume**
Recordings made with clean digital tools that add analog artifacts afterward — vinyl crackle, tape hiss, slight pitch wobble — to suggest age or intimacy they do not possess. The surface should be a consequence of the process, not a design choice applied at the end. Fake lo-fi is performance, not material.

**Nature documentary texture**
Ambient that sounds like underscore for wildlife footage. Beautiful in its context. Without that context, without the image it was made to accompany, it has nothing to say. Music designed to illustrate something else is not music for this label.

**Spotify ambient**
Releases produced specifically for algorithmic placement on sleep, focus, study, or relaxation playlists. These releases are identifiable by their duration (exactly 2 minutes, or exactly 10 minutes), their absence of dynamic range, their content-neutral texture, and the fact that nothing changes for the entire duration. This is not music. It is content.

**Generative ambient without authorship**
Algorithmic or generative works where the composer designed a system and let it run — without the critical editing, compositional revision, and intentional selection that converts output into work. The process is not the product. The machine's output requires a human decision about what to keep and what to discard. If that decision was not made, or made carelessly, the result is not a release.

**Dark ambient as genre signal**
Music that uses darkness — dissonant tones, industrial texture, low-end pressure — as genre signaling rather than expression. The problem is not darkness. Darkness is welcome here. The problem is music where the darkness is communicating "I am dark ambient" rather than anything else. Genre costume instead of position.

---

## AI-GENERATED MUSIC

The position here is the same as on every label that takes music seriously.

AI generates patterns that resemble music. The absence of a body, a life, a specific formation, a set of decisions made under specific constraints at a specific moment in a specific place — this is not a technical limitation that better models will eventually overcome. It is what music is. Music carries the specific gravity of a specific existence. An AI has no existence to carry.

The Moon Records uses AI as operational infrastructure: documentation, automation, workflow. That is not a contradiction. A microphone is also infrastructure. The microphone does not make the music.

AI-generated submissions will not be evaluated.

---

## ARTIST TYPES THAT DO NOT FIT

**The ambient-as-rest artist**
Artists who make ambient music in the gaps between other projects — when there is nothing else to release, when the beat-based material is not ready, when the ambient "just came together." For this artist, ambient is the default, not the choice. The music usually sounds like it. Ambient made without specific commitment to what ambient is — as a form, as a discipline, as a decision — is ambient as filler, regardless of what else the artist does.

**The tool-first artist**
Artists whose primary relationship is to a software environment or a specific piece of hardware, and whose music is primarily a demonstration of what that tool can do. The tool is visible in the music, not the person. No amount of technical sophistication substitutes for a compositional position.

**The genre-adjacent artist**
Artists who make music that is technically beatless but whose primary influences, aesthetics, and sonic references are from rhythmic music. The ambient work is adjacent to something else. It sounds like a techno track with the kick removed, or a hip-hop beat underneath a long pad. The removal of rhythm did not produce ambient music. It produced music with a missing element.

---

## A NOTE ON COLLABORATION

The exclusion criteria above apply to the work, not the artist as a complete entity. An artist who also produces techno, or who has released rhythmic work elsewhere, is not excluded. The specific work submitted for The Moon Records must meet the criteria. What an artist does in their other capacities is irrelevant to the A&R decision.
